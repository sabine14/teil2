More about the Mani: wikipedia,

The Mani Peninsula (Greek: Μάνη) is a geographical and cultural region in southern Greece, it is the central peninsula of the three which extend southwards from the Peloponnese. In the early modern period, Maniots(the inhabitants) had a reputation as fierce and proudly independent warriors, who engaged in piracy and fierce blood feuds.

The Maniots lived in fortified villages and tall stone towers. About 800 towers are scattered all over Mani, some stone towers were many-storeyed, they are 20 meters high and have from 4 to 5 floors. The towers were equipped with loopholes, boilers for water and special places at tower corners for throwing stones at enemies. Due to towers Mani managed to save its freedom and independance.

Stones are one of the natural resource of the Mani, the other are olives. Mani is known for what some consider the world's best extra-virgin olive oil, soft-pressed from partially ripened olives of the Koroneiki variety, which are organically grown on mountain terraces.
